Paneer Butter Masala

DESCRIPTION:

Paneer butter masala, also known as butter paneer, is a popular Indian dish featuring soft paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes in a rich and creamy gravy. This mild, sweet, and tangy curry is known for its luxurious texture and flavor.

Paneer butter masala is a vegetarian delight, often considered the vegetarian equivalent of butter chicken. It's a staple in Indian restaurants and households, enjoyed for its comforting appeal and satisfying taste. This dish pairs well with Indian breads like naan, roti, or kulcha, as well as with rice dishes like plain or jeera rice. While it's known for its richness and caloric content, it can be made healthier by opting for low-fat dairy products and incorporating vegetables like bell peppers or peas.

INGREDIENTS:

  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ pound paneer, cut into ½-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon ground cashew
  • 1 teaspoon ground red chillies
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ½ cup half-and-half
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt

STEPS:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat; fry paneer in batches until golden, about 5 minutes.
  3. Transfer fried paneer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain, retaining vegetable oil in skillet.
  4. Melt butter in the same skillet over medium heat; cook and stir onion until golden brown, about 10 minutes.
  5. Add ginger paste and garlic paste. Continue to cook until fragrant, about 1 minute more.
  6. Stir cashews, ground red chiles, cumin, coriander, and garam masala into the onion mixture. Cook and stir for 1 minute.
  7. Stir tomato sauce, half-and-half, milk, sugar, and salt into spice mixture; simmer until thickened, about 5 minutes.
  8. Reduce heat to low. Add fried paneer and simmer until heated through, about 5 minutes more.
  9. And now you can serve this paneer butter masala, while its hot with some butter garlic nan.
